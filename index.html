About

I'm a Computer Science & Engineering (Cyber Security) student at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Delhi NCR, and a young ethical hacker with hands-on experience in SOC operations, network security monitoring, vulnerability assessment, and security compliance documentation.

My independent security research centers on responsible vulnerability disclosure — identifying access-control, misconfiguration, and IAM issues in public-facing web applications and reporting them through coordinated channels like CERT-In and HackerOne. As a student security researcher, I've tried to treat each finding as documentation-worthy, not just a report to file and forget.

That work was featured in Dark Reading in June 2026 and recognized with a CERT-In Hall of Fame listing. More recently, I've been exploring the intersection of cybersecurity and artificial intelligence — experimenting with AI-assisted security tooling and reading into the emerging fields of AI security and AI safety research, alongside continuing disclosure work and public writing on the path into security research.

Approach

Most of my work as an ethical hacker starts the same way: recon, careful enumeration, and manual testing rather than relying purely on automated scanners. I'd rather find one well-documented, high-confidence issue than a long list of low-signal noise.

As a young security researcher still early in my career, I try to be deliberate about what I publish — writing up root causes, not just proof-of-concept screenshots, and thinking about what a defender or another researcher could actually learn from a disclosure.

That same instinct is pulling me toward AI security and AI safety research: as more security tooling and decision-making gets built on top of AI systems, understanding how those systems fail — and how to test them responsibly — feels like a natural extension of the disclosure work I already do.
